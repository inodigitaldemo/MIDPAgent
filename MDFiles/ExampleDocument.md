# Document Management According to ISO 9001

## Overview

ISO 9001 requires organizations to maintain a documented quality management system (QMS). Effective document control ensures that only current, approved versions of documents are in use, and that obsolete documents are withdrawn from circulation.

## Key Requirements

ISO 9001:2015 Clause 7.5 outlines the requirements for documented information, covering creation, updating, and control of documents. Organizations must ensure documents are available, suitable for use, and adequately protected.

## Document Control Process

| Step | Activity | Responsible |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Create or draft document | Document Owner |
| 2 | Review for accuracy and completeness | Subject Matter Expert |
| 3 | Approve and authorize release | Quality Manager |
| 4 | Distribute to relevant personnel | Document Controller |
| 5 | Archive previous version | Document Controller |
| 6 | Review and update periodically | Document Owner |

## Document Retention Requirements

| Document Type | Minimum Retention Period | Format |
|---------------|--------------------------|--------|
| Quality Policy | Duration of QMS + 3 years | Electronic / Hard copy |
| Audit Reports | 3 years | Electronic |
| Nonconformance Records | 5 years | Electronic |
| Training Records | Duration of employment + 3 years | Electronic / Hard copy |
| Customer Complaints | 5 years | Electronic |

## Best Practices

- Assign a unique document identifier and version number to every controlled document.
- Use a Document Master Register to track all active documents and their current revision status.
- Ensure documents are reviewed at defined intervals (typically annually) to confirm continued suitability.
- Restrict editing rights to authorized personnel to prevent unauthorized changes.
- Maintain clear records of changes between document versions to support traceability.
